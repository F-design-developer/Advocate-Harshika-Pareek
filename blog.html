Labour Law

Periodic Labour Force Survey (PLFS) 2018-19 Analysis

Comprehensive analysis of employment, unemployment, labour participation and literacy trends in India.

Introduction

The National Statistical Office conducted PLFS from July 2018 to June 2019 to provide labour market indicators related to employment and unemployment in India.

Salient Features
  • 12,720 First Stage Sampling Units covered.
  • 6,983 Rural & 5,737 Urban Units.
  • 4,20,757 Individuals surveyed.
  • 51.7% Rural Self Employment.
  • 25.1% Casual Labour.
  • 13.1% Regular Wage Earners.
Government Policies
  • Atma Nirbhar Bharat Abhiyan
  • PM SVANidhi
  • PM-KISAN
  • MGNREGA
  • National Career Service
  • Deen Dayal Antyodaya Yojana
Critical Analysis

Labour Force Participation Rate increased from 36.9% to 37.5% while unemployment declined from 6.1% to 5.8%. Female participation and literacy also improved.

Conclusion

PLFS provides valuable annual and quarterly labour market data helping policymakers understand employment trends and economic conditions.

Criminal Law

Laws Regarding Mob Lynching In India

Legal perspective on mob lynching, constitutional rights, Supreme Court guidelines and reforms.

Lynching Problem

Mob lynching refers to extrajudicial killing by a group. Causes include fake news, communal tension, political motives and social conflicts.

Legal Provisions
  • Special Nodal Officers
  • Monitoring of Fake News
  • Fast Track Courts
  • Victim Compensation Schemes
  • Police Accountability
  • Section 153A IPC
State Initiatives
  • Manipur Anti-Lynching Law
  • West Bengal Anti-Lynching Law
  • Rajasthan Anti-Lynching Law
Scope Of Improvement

Stronger implementation, public awareness, strict punishment and accountability mechanisms are necessary.

Conclusion

Citizens must trust the justice system rather than taking law into their own hands.
